Unfortunately, golden retrievers are not considered a hypoallergenic breed. In fact, they're actually notorious for causing allergic reactions in humans. This is because golden retrievers shed a lot and produce a significant amount of dander, which can trigger allergies. Dander is the dead skin cells that animals shed, and it's one of the most common allergens for humans. When animals shed, these tiny particles of dander become airborne and can trigger an allergic reaction in a sensitive individual. This is why people with allergies are often advised to avoid pets altogether.
Despite the fact that golden retrievers are not hypoallergenic, there are steps that can be taken
to reduce the likelihood of experiencing an allergic reaction. Regular grooming is essential for golden
retrievers as it can help to control shedding and reduce the amount of dander in the environment.
Bathing your dog once a week or more can also help to wash away any allergens on their coat or skin.
