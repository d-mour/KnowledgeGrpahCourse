Many people mistakenly believe that dog hair is the main culprit of allergies.
However, it is not the hair itself, but the dander, urine, and saliva that cause allergic reactions. Dander is made up of tiny flecks of skin that are shed by animals,
and it contains the allergens that can trigger allergies in sensitive individuals.
Therefore, even dogs with short hair can still be problematic for those with allergies.
When it comes to Pembroke Welsh Corgis, unfortunately, they are not considered a hypoallergenic breed.
These dogs have a thick double coat that sheds year-round and requires regular maintenance.
The shedding process can release a significant amount of dander into the environment,
making it more challenging for allergy sufferers to live with this breed.
Although they are small in size, their shedding can be quite extensive,
leading to allergic reactions in sensitive individuals or causing inconvenience for those who prefer a cleaner environment.
Furthermore, Pembroke Welsh Corgis have a tendency to drool, which can also contribute to allergic reactions.
Their saliva contains allergenic proteins that can cause itching, watery eyes, sneezing,
and even asthma attacks in susceptible individuals.
Therefore, if a person is allergic to pet dander and also sensitive to dog saliva,
living with a Pembroke Welsh Corgi might not be the best choice.
