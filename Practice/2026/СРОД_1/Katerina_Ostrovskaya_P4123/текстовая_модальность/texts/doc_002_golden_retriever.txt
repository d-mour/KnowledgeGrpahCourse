The Golden Retriever is a beloved family dog known for its friendly and gentle temperament. It has a dense, water-repellent double coat that sheds heavily, especially during spring and autumn. This heavy shedding releases large amounts of hair and dander into the air and onto furniture. Dander carries the Can f1 protein, which is the main cause of dog-related allergic reactions in humans. People who suffer from allergies or asthma are generally advised to avoid Golden Retrievers, as exposure can trigger sneezing, itchy eyes, and breathing difficulties. The Golden Retriever is not considered a hypoallergenic breed.
