The Persian is one of the oldest and most recognizable cat breeds, known for its long, silky coat and flat face. Persians require daily grooming due to their extremely long fur, which sheds consistently throughout the year. The shedding spreads the Fel d1 protein — the allergen produced in the cat's saliva and sebaceous glands — across the home environment. As a result, Persian cats are not recommended for people with cat allergies. Allergy sufferers who live with a Persian often experience frequent sneezing, skin irritation, and eye inflammation. The Persian is not a hypoallergenic breed.
